Our Mission

History demands a witness. We build one.

The Memorial Project is a nonprofit at the intersection of AI and Holocaust remembrance. We partner with institutions worldwide — archives, museums, education centers — to transform fragile testimonies into permanent, searchable, educational resources.

As the generation of survivors passes, the urgency of our work compounds. Every transcript, photograph, and recorded voice is a thread connecting the present to one of history's darkest chapters. We are here to hold those threads.

How We Work

Three pillars of remembrance.

I

Archive & Digitize

We work with leading institutions — including Yad Vashem, the USHMM, the Arolsen Archives, the VHEC, and Europeana — to ingest, structure, and semantically enrich fragile historical records. Photographs, documents, testimonies, and oral histories become searchable, cross-referenced knowledge.

II

Animate & Humanize

AI allows us to go beyond static records. We generate first-person narratives, AI-assisted memorial sites, and interactive testimony experiences. Survivors speak not just through transcripts — but through living digital presences that educators and families can engage with directly.

III

Educate & Distribute

Memory without transmission dies. We build curriculum tools, integrate with platforms like Google Classroom and NotebookLM, and produce content that reaches students from elementary through university. Our mission is not just to store history — it is to teach it, generation after generation.

Technology & Infrastructure

Built for permanence.
